Chapter 73: The Elemental Drawback

发布时间：2026/6/30 13:58:27

第七十三章：元素性的弊端

The hideout was an assorted mess of ruined bookshelf units and water-damaged pictorial magazines. Fujiwara sat among them, posing as a thinker, though he was merely an absentee from his own sanity. His metabolic rate had slowed to a crawl, mirroring the modernist decay of the city outside. He had become addicted to the obnoxious hum of a broken air purifier, a negligent guardian of their dwindling valuables.

这个藏身处是一堆乱七八糟的破烂书架和受潮的画报。藤原坐在其中，装出一副思想家的样子，其实他只是从自己的理智中缺席了。他的代谢速度慢如蜗牛，映射出外界那现代主义的腐朽。他染上了对一台坏掉的空气净化器那刺耳嗡嗡声的瘾，成了一个对自己逐渐缩水的财产毫无责任感的守卫者。

Miyetsuko, the co author of their ruin, was privately plotting how to disseminate chaos. She had a strong inclination to marginalize anyone who questioned her inconsistent strategies. She began an engaging monologue about the zoology of the local neo nazi patrols, who were famously persecute-ing the remaining pluralist intellectuals. "They personify the elemental cruelty of our age," she spat, tossing a bundle of asparagus into a pot of herbicide-tainted water. "The drawback of being a pro life advocate in a graveyard, one might say."

作为他们毁灭的共同作者，美悦子正私下策划着如何散播混乱。她极度倾向于排斥任何质疑她前后矛盾策略的人。她开始了一段关于当地新纳粹巡逻队动物学的迷人独白，这些纳粹以迫害残存的多元主义知识分子而闻名。“他们体现了这个时代那最原始的残酷，”她啐了一口，把一把芦笋扔进了一锅被除草剂污染的水里。“有人可能会说，在坟场里做一个维护生命权利的倡导者，本身就是个巨大的弊端。”

Sayuri, suffering from the pneumonia of a lonely heart and the onset of menopause, spent her time playing a clarinet that only produced a flat, wheezing sound. She was presently obsessing over a drugstore she had visited in a dream. It was an old style place, plentiful with remedies that could cure her reproductive sorrow. Rin, however, was busy with a hand held scanner, trying to duplicate the signature of a dead consulate official. She found the local snobbery towards their survival tactics to be unconstitutional, a blatant obscenity against their rights.

小百合忍受着孤独心脏带来的肺炎和更年期，她花时间吹奏着一把只能发出扁平、嘶哑声的单簧管。她此时正沉迷于一个梦中去过的药店。那是一家老式的店，有着充足的能治愈她生殖忧伤的药物。然而，铃正忙着使用一台手持扫描仪，试图伪造一名已故领事馆官员的签名。她发现当地人对他们生存策略的势利态度是违宪的，是对他们权利赤裸裸的亵渎。

"The pertinent data," Rin announced, her voice as dry as the pneumonia-ridden air, "is that we are exemplify-ing failure. Even the colonist patrols know it. They are coming to hamstring our last exit."

“相关数据，”铃宣布，声音像那充满了肺炎病毒的空气一样干涩，“表明我们正成为失败的例证。连那些殖民者巡逻队都知道。他们正准备切断我们最后的退路。”

Fujiwara stood up, clutching a hand held duplicate of their shopping list. "Is this anecdotal?" he asked, pointing to a smudge. "Or is it the firsthand mark of the end?"

藤原站起身，紧紧抓住那张购物清单的手持副本。“这是轶事？”他指着上面的污迹问道，“还是末日的亲身印记？”

"It's just halloween for the damned," Miyetsuko sighed, ignoring the obnoxious rattling of the door. She knew the end was coming, and she found it rather engaging.

“这只是给该死之人的万圣节，”美悦子叹了口气，无视门那刺耳的撞击声。她知道终局将至，而且她觉得这还挺有趣的。

[Learning Point]

Negligent: 疏忽的/不在意的。

Disseminate: 散播/传播。

Pertinent: 相关的/切题的。

Hamstring: 切断后路/束缚。

Unconstitutional: 违宪的。
