Chapter 72: The Constellation of Crumbling Laws

发布时间：2026/6/30 13:38:39

第七十二章：崩塌法则的星群

The nursing home had become a totalitarian space station of despair, floating in an incompatible reality. Fujiwara sat by an air conditioned vent, trying to find the practicality in an unsatisfactory shopping list that included chrysanthemum extract and electromagnetic stickers. His daughter in law—a conscientious dropout from the Open University—had left him a driving licence that was clearly a cover version of the real thing, an infringement of every authoritarian state of the art regulation.

这家养老院已变成了一座绝望的极权主义空间站，漂浮在不兼容的现实中。藤原坐在冷气排风口旁，试图从一份写着菊花提取物和电磁贴纸的令人不满的购物清单中找到实用性。他的儿媳——一位从开放大学辍学的尽职弃学者——给他留了一张驾驶执照，那显然是一份赝品，是对所有威权主义尖端法规的侵犯。

"It’s preposterous," Miyetsuko declared, her journalistic comprehension of the ramifications of their metaphysical inconsistency growing daily. She was drafting a collaborative introductory statement for a multilateral subcommittee on biotechnology and prostitution. "The chronological inconsistency of our occupational reproduction is a prerequisite for the collapse," she noted, her tone confrontational. "We are living in a transitional constellation of unconvincing lies."

“这太荒谬了，”美悦子宣布，她对他们形而上学的不一致性所带来的后果的理解与日俱增。她正在为生物技术与卖淫问题的多边小组委员会起草一份协作性的介绍声明。“我们职业化再生产在时间顺序上的不一致，是崩塌的先决条件，”她语气充满对抗性地指出，“我们生活在一个由难以令人信服的谎言组成的过渡性星群中。”

Sayuri, meanwhile, was obsessed with a quantitative analysis of her self confidence, despite a dermatologist’s warning about tuberculosis scars and osteoporosis. She wore a three dimensional wasp-shaped brooch, a tsar-like accessory that felt inflammatory to the presbyterian staff. "The idiosyncratic dissatisfaction," she sighed, "is purely recreational." She spent her days at the local shopping centre, near the polling station, attempting to substantiate her claims with stickers found in a food processor.

小百合则沉迷于对自己自信心的定量分析，尽管皮肤科医生警告过她关于肺结核疤痕和骨质疏松的风险。她戴着一枚三维黄蜂形状的胸针，那配件像沙皇般傲慢，令那些长老会的员工感到愤慨。“这种古怪的不满，”她叹道，“纯粹是消遣。”她终日流连于投票站附近的购物中心，试图用从食品加工机里找到的贴纸来证实她的主张。

Rin, acting as the law enforcement of their elementary school-level housing benefit scheme, paced the assembly line of their shared bedroom. She was manipulative in her idiosyncratic complementary logic, constantly checking the number plate of every aircraft carrier-sized ghost that drifted by. "The quantitative inconsistency of our housing benefit," she whispered, "is indefinitely linked to the nuclear reactor beneath the nursing home."

铃作为他们小学级住房补贴方案的执法者，在共用卧室的装配线上踱步。她以一种古怪而互补的逻辑进行着操纵，不断检查着每一个漂浮而过的航空母舰般巨大的鬼魂的牌照。“我们住房补贴在数量上的不一致，”她低语道，“与养老院底下的核反应堆有着无限的联系。”

"Is the tsar watching?" Fujiwara asked, his self confidence waning.

“那位沙皇在监视吗？”藤原问，自信心正在减弱。

"The tsar is an identifiable idiosyncratic constellation," Miyetsuko replied, her eyes fixed on a manipulative journalistic snapshot. "And we are merely the stickers on a preposterous nuclear reactor."

“沙皇只是一个可识别的怪异星群，”美悦子回答，双眼紧盯着一张操纵性极强的新闻快照。“而我们，只不过是一个荒谬核反应堆上的贴纸。”

[Learning Point]

Totalitarian: 极权主义的。

Ramification: 后果/分支。

Inflammatory: 煽动性的/易燃的。

Idiosyncratic: 古怪的/特质的。

Substantiate: 证实/实体化。
