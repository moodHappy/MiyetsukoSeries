Chapter 60: The Relocation of Rot

发布时间：2026/6/26 14:28:50

第六十章：搬运腐坏的圣殿

The departure was less of a move and more of an eviction of their own accumulated toxicity. Their new home—a windowless concrete bunker three miles further from the "Fresh Mart"—offered no luxury, only a different set of walls to rot within.

这场搬离与其说是搬家，不如说是一次对自身堆积出的毒素的驱逐。新家是个离“新鲜玛特”三英里远、没有窗户的混凝土掩体，那里没有任何奢华可言，只有另一堵供他们继续腐烂的墙。

Miyetsuko stood in the center of the chaos, waving her arms with the frantic, autocratic precision of a general directing a retreat from a losing war.

美悦子伫立在混乱中心，挥舞着手臂，指挥姿态犹如一位将军在溃败的战争中组织撤退，既疯狂又独断。

"Watch the corners!" she snapped. "If you scratch the paint, I’m deducting it from your ration paste!"

“看好边角！”她厉声喝道，“要是敢蹭掉漆，我从你们的配给膏里扣！”

Fujiwara groaned under the weight of the store’s industrial freezer. It was leaking a viscous, neon-green slime, and the interior was packed tight with jars of fermented kimchi that had turned into a pressurized, acidic gas-bomb.

藤原扛着那台工业冷柜，发出沉重的呻吟。柜底渗出黏稠的荧光绿污液，里面塞满了发酵到足以成为酸性压力炸弹的过期泡菜。

"Move, Fujiwara," Sayuri gasped, stumbling past him. She was struggling to carry a mountain of silicone artifacts—the heavy, complex machinery of her profession. Her legs were shaky, the inner thighs inflamed and raw from a week of "intensive maintenance." With every step, a steady trickle of clear fluid escaped her, drip-drip-drip, soaking into the cardboard boxes.

“快走，藤原，”小百合气喘吁吁地挤过他。她怀抱着如山般的硅胶制品——那些属于她职业生涯的复杂重器。她的双腿在颤抖，大腿内侧因一周的“高强度维护”而红肿不堪。每走一步，清澈的体液便淅淅沥沥地滴落，在搬家用的纸盒上印出一圈圈水渍。

Gush. Gurgle. Squelch.

“咕叽，咕叽。”

"Careful, Sayuri," Miyetsuko teased, not looking up from her list. "You’re lubricating the floor for the scavengers. If you keep leaking, we’ll have to charge you a floor-maintenance fee."

“小心点，小百合，”美悦子头也不抬地调侃，“你把地板润滑得太好了。照这么滴下去，我们得收你地板养护费。”

Sayuri let out a jagged laugh, pausing to adjust a vibrating device that was buzzing uncontrollably. "It’s not my fault. These things... they have a memory of their own. They don't want to leave."

小百合发出尖锐的笑声，停下来调整那件震动失控的器具。“不是我的错，这些东西……它们自己有记忆。它们不想走。”

In the bathroom, the air was thick enough to chew. Rin was on her knees, scrubbing at the crusty, yellowed stains on the tiles with a frayed mop.

浴室里，空气浓稠得近乎固态。铃跪在地上，用一把烂拖把拼命刷洗瓷砖上那层干涸、泛黄的污垢。

"It’s not clean, Rin," Fujiwara muttered, dropping the freezer with a metallic thud. "It hasn't been clean since 2240. Stop pretending."

“别擦了，铃，”藤原把冷柜往地上一扔，发出金属重击声，“自2240年起这儿就没干净过，别自欺欺人了。”

Rin didn't stop. "It’s the principle, Fujiwara. The Saint left his mark here. I’m just... archiving it."

铃没停手。“这是原则问题，藤原。圣子留下的痕迹，我得……存个档。”

She and Sayuri carefully detached the toilet bowl, a porcelain wreck stained with the "Toilet Saint’s" erratic biological legacy. They handled it with the reverence one might reserve for a religious relic, gently wrapping it in a mink stole before nestling it into an expensive, brass-latched designer suitcase.

她们小心翼翼地拆下马桶——那件沾染着“马桶圣子”生物遗产的陶瓷废墟。她们将其视若宗教圣物，先用貂皮披肩小心包裹，再轻手轻脚地塞进一只带铜锁的名牌皮箱里。

The doorbell rang—or rather, a rhythmic banging on the metal frame signaled the arrival of the moving crew. They were a motley group of scavengers, their bodies reconstructed with rusted metal, half-limbs, and scavenged wiring.

门铃响了——确切说是有人在金属框上疯狂砸击。搬家工人们到了。这是一群拾荒者，躯干上打着锈迹斑斑的金属补丁，装配着半截义肢和不知从哪拼凑的线缆。

"Move it, meat-bags," the lead scavenger grunted, his voice sounding like gravel in a blender. "We got a quota to meet before the acid rain starts."

“快点，肉块们，”领头的工人大声嘟囔，嗓音像搅拌机里的碎石，“酸雨降下来之前，老子还得凑够绩效。”

"We have delicate cargo," Miyetsuko declared, pointing to the designer suitcase containing the stained toilet.

“我们有精密货物，”美悦子指着那只装有马桶的名牌箱子宣布。

The scavenger looked at the suitcase, then at the leaking freezer, then at Sayuri, who was still dripping onto the boxes. He let out a long, wet, mechanical wheeze of a laugh.

工人们看看皮箱，看看漏水的冷柜，又看看正往纸盒上滴水的小百合。他发出一阵漫长、湿润且机械的冷笑。

"Delicate, huh?" he spat. "Lady, in this city, the only thing 'delicate' left is the time before we all turn into paste. Toss it on the pile."

“精密？”他啐了一口，“大妈，在这座城里，唯一称得上‘精密’的，就是咱们变成膏浆前剩下的那点时间。给老子扔车斗里去。”

[Learning Point]

Eviction: 驱逐/收回（此处指迁离）。

Viscous: 黏稠的。

Reverence: 崇敬。

Motley: 杂乱的/混杂的。

Scavenger: 拾荒者。
